# The Dual Transport Model

## Why Two Sugars Are Better Than One

Your small intestine absorbs carbohydrates through specialized protein channels called transporters. The key insight behind modern sports nutrition: **glucose and fructose use completely different transporters**.

## The Two Pathways

### SGLT1 -- The Glucose Channel

- **Sodium-Glucose Linked Transporter 1**
- Located on the brush border of enterocytes (small intestine cells)
- Absorbs glucose and glucose polymers (maltodextrin, starch)
- Sodium-dependent: co-transports Na+ and water (bonus for hydration)
- **Saturates at approximately 60 g/h** -- this is a hard physiological ceiling

### GLUT5 -- The Fructose Channel

- **Facilitative Glucose Transporter 5**
- Completely independent pathway from SGLT1
- Absorbs fructose via facilitated diffusion (no sodium required)
- Provides an additional ~30-40 g/h absorption capacity

## The Bottleneck Problem

For decades, researchers observed that exogenous carbohydrate oxidation never exceeded ~1 g/min (60 g/h), regardless of how much glucose athletes consumed. More glucose simply accumulated in the intestinal lumen, causing bloating and GI distress.

The breakthrough came when Jentjens & Jeukendrup (2005) demonstrated that **combining glucose with fructose** bypassed the SGLT1 bottleneck entirely. By recruiting a second transporter, total absorption increased by up to 75%.

## Practical Implication

| Intake Target | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|
| < 60 g/h | Any carbohydrate source works (glucose, maltodextrin, sucrose) |
| 60-90 g/h | **Must** use glucose + fructose combination |
| 90-120 g/h | Glucose + fructose with trained gut required |

> Maltodextrin (a glucose polymer) behaves identically to glucose in terms of transporter use -- it is rapidly broken down to glucose before absorption via SGLT1.

## Key References

- Jentjens & Jeukendrup (2005). *Int J Sport Nutr Exerc Metab* -- First demonstration of oxidation rates above 1 g/min with glucose + fructose
- Jeukendrup (2010). *Curr Opin Clin Nutr Metab Care* -- Review confirming 75% higher oxidation with multiple transportable carbohydrates
- Jeukendrup (2004). *Nutrition* -- Establishing the ~1 g/min limit for single carbohydrate sources
