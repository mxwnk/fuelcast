# Timing and Dosing

## When, How Much, How Often

The timing and distribution of carbohydrate intake during exercise significantly affects both absorption efficiency and GI comfort.

## When to Start

- Begin fueling within the **first 15-30 minutes** of exercise lasting >60 min
- Peak oxidation of ingested carbohydrates takes **75-90 minutes** to reach maximum
- Starting early avoids a "fuel gap" in the middle of your race
- Pre-exercise carbohydrate loading and during-exercise feeding complement each other (Febbraio et al., 2000)

## Feeding Frequency

**Little and often beats large and infrequent:**

- Every **15-20 minutes** with smaller doses is preferred
- Maintains more stable blood glucose levels
- Reduces GI burden per serving
- Fielding et al. (1985): feeding every 30 min outperformed every 60 min at the same total dose

**Practical cadence examples (at 80 g/h):**
- 1 gel (25 g) every ~19 min
- 1 gel (40 g) every ~30 min
- Sips from a bottle between gels

## Duration-Based Guidelines

Based on Jeukendrup (2014):

| Duration | Recommendation | Carb Type |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| < 45 min | None needed (mouth rinse may help) | Any |
| 45-75 min | Small amounts or mouth rinse | Any |
| 1-2 h | 30-60 g/h | Single source OK |
| 2-3 h | 60-90 g/h | Multiple transportable CHO |
| > 3 h | 80-120 g/h | Multiple transportable CHO required |

## Gastric Emptying

- Solutions of **6-8% carbohydrate** concentration optimize delivery vs. emptying speed
- Concentrations above 8% slow gastric emptying significantly
- Larger fluid volumes in the stomach actually empty faster (in absolute terms)
- Gastric emptying slows above ~70% VO2max as blood is diverted to working muscles
- Cool fluids (10-15 degrees C) may empty slightly faster than warm

## Intensity Considerations

- **< 50% VO2max:** Carb demands are low; reduce intake accordingly
- **60-75% VO2max:** Standard recommendations apply
- **> 80% VO2max:** Maximal CHO reliance, but GI tolerance may decrease due to reduced splanchnic blood flow

## Key References

- Fielding et al. (1985). *Med Sci Sports Exerc* -- Frequent feeding > infrequent feeding
- Jeukendrup (2014). *Sports Med* -- Duration-based guidelines
- ISSN Position Stand (2017). *JISSN* -- 6-8% CHO solution every 10-15 min for exercise >60 min
- Febbraio et al. (2000). *J Appl Physiol* -- Pre-exercise + during-exercise feeding maximizes performance
