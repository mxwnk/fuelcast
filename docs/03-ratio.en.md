# The Optimal Ratio

## Glucose:Fructose -- Finding the Sweet Spot

The ratio between glucose (or maltodextrin) and fructose determines how efficiently both intestinal transporters are utilized during high-rate carbohydrate fueling.

## The Classic 2:1 Ratio

Established by Jeukendrup's laboratory through a series of studies in the mid-2000s:

- At 90 g/h intake: delivers ~60 g glucose (saturating SGLT1) + ~30 g fructose (utilizing GLUT5)
- Both transport pathways are activated without overwhelming either
- Most commercial sports nutrition products use this ratio
- Extensively validated in performance studies

## The Emerging 1:0.8 Ratio

More recent research suggests higher fructose fractions may be equally effective:

- O'Brien et al. (2013) and Rowlands et al. (2015) found comparable oxidation rates and performance with ratios closer to 1:0.8 or even 1:1
- Fructose absorption capacity may be higher than originally estimated
- Some modern products (e.g., newer gel formulations) are adopting this ratio

## When Does the Ratio Matter?

**Above 60 g/h:** The ratio becomes critical because SGLT1 is at or near saturation. Any excess glucose has nowhere to go.

**Below 60 g/h:** The ratio is less important because SGLT1 still has spare capacity. Even pure glucose works fine at these intake rates.

## Practical Mixing Examples (for 90 g/h)

| Ratio | Glucose/Maltodextrin | Fructose | Total |
|-------|---------------------|----------|-------|
| 2:1 | 60 g | 30 g | 90 g |
| 1:0.8 | 50 g | 40 g | 90 g |
| 1:1 (sucrose-based) | 45 g | 45 g | 90 g |

> Note: Sucrose (table sugar) is digested into a 1:1 glucose:fructose mix. It is a viable, cheap dual-transport carbohydrate source for moderate intake rates.

## Recommendation

- **2:1 is the safe, well-validated default.** Most research supports it.
- **1:0.8 is viable** for athletes who tolerate higher fructose well.
- The exact ratio is less critical than ensuring **both transporters are recruited** at high intake rates.

## Key References

- Jentjens et al. (2004a,b). *J Appl Physiol* -- Established 2:1 as optimal
- Rowlands et al. (2015). *Med Sci Sports Exerc* -- 1:0.8 showed similar oxidation and performance
- O'Brien et al. (2013). *Scand J Med Sci Sports* -- Comparable results with higher fructose fractions
- Jeukendrup (2014). *Sports Med* -- Recommends glucose:fructose at 2:1 for ultra-events
